Taking x! to be the integral of t^x/e^t dt over all positive t, as is often done, is there any simple/easy way to establish the asymptotic that (n + x)! ~ n! n^x for fixed x and large n (thus equating the integral and infinite product definitions of the generalized factorial)?

@loundcushblount At the moment, the best I've got is the observation that the integral of t^x (1 - t/n)^n dt over t in [0, n] is n^(x + 1) * n!/((x + 1) * (x + 2) * ... (x + n + 1)). Taking the limit as n → ∞, the LHS becomes the integral def and the RHS the infinite product def of x!.
