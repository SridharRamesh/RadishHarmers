And so on. In N dimensions, N such tapering figures fill an N-dimensional cube, so each such tapering figure has size 1/N times that of the cube. This tells us that slanted tapering figures of unit height and unit base size have size 1/N.

That is, regardless of slant details, the cross-sections of a tapering figure grow linearly as we move from tip to base. And all that matters for total size is an aggregation of cross-section sizes (this is called Cavalieri's principle).
