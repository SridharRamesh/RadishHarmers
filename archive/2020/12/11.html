@littmath If A^(p^k) - 1 = 0, then by Frobenius (A - 1)^(p^k) = 0, so the minimal polynomial is (A - 1)^m for some m at most p^k. Reversely, if the minimal polynomial is (A - 1)^m, then letting p^k be at least m, we have that (A - 1)^(p^k) = 0, and thus by Frobenius A^(p^k) - 1 = 0.
