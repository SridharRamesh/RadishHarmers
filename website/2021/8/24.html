@antihydra1 What does it mean for log(B) to be the derivative of B^x at x = 0? It means that B^ε is about the same as B^0 + log(B)ε = 1 + log(B)ε for small ε.

@antihydra1 Each of the binomial coefficients (N choose k) here is a degree k polynomial of N, whose leading coefficient is 1/k!.

For example, (N choose 3) is N * (N - 1) * (N - 2) / 3!.

Therefore, each (N choose k)/N^k approaches 1/k! as N grows large.
