@donorsHATEhim A simple example of a well-ordered set which goes "further than" N is taking all the indices in N, and adding one new index greater than all of them.

A bigger example is taking two copies of N, one after the other.

@donorsHATEhim An even bigger example is taking N many copies of N, one after another, in the sense of taking pairs of natural numbers, compared first by their first element, and then if they match on their first element compared by their second element.
