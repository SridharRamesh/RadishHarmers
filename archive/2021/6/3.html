On the Mth round of deletions, the people who are deleted are precisely those for which the longest path ending in them is M people long. So the people who survive to the end are precisely those for which there is no longest path ending in them.

Since there's only N many people around total, this path must have some repetition of people in it, meaning it goes through a cycle. The path just keeps running around that cycle once it enters it, and we chose this path so that it ends in person P, so person P is in that cycle.


Timestamp: 2021-6-3 4:52:1
Id: 1400314306030452736

And conversely, in our N person world, if there's no longest path ending in person P, then person P is in a cycle. Why is that? Well, start off by picking some path of length more than N people that ends in P.


Timestamp: 2021-6-3 4:52:1
Id: 1400314305074106369

Note that any person in a cycle has no longest path ending in them, since we can keep going around such a cycle for as long as we want.


Timestamp: 2021-6-3 4:52:1

Keep in mind, there's no rule against a path repeating people. Indeed, this concept is important. By a "cycle", I mean a path that starts at some person, and then loops back to that same starting person.


Timestamp: 2021-6-3 4:52:1

In general, a structure with one "root" person who doesn't follow anyone, plus other people each of whom follows precisely one person, with everyone having a path to the root, is called a "tree".

So what we're looking for is like a tree, but modified so the root self-follows.

Now, we pick a random person who hasn't chosen who to follow yet, and also pick a random person out of everyone in the world, and make the former follow the latter. Put another way, we pick a random person in the world to be a followee, and a random root to be their follower.

So the probability of success for our first choice will be (N - 1) / N, when no one has yet chosen who to follow. Then after that, the probability of maintaining success with our second choice will be (N - 2) / (N - 1). Then the next factor will be (N - 3) / (N - 2), and so on.


Timestamp: 2021-6-3 5:6:45

Timestamp: 2021-6-3 5:6:45

I.e., for any particular choice of follower, when it comes to choices of followee, there will be R roots available, but only R - 1 of them avoid screwing us up. So we have probability (R - 1) / R of making it through this choice without screwing ourselves into making a cycle.
