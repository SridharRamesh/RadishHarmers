@emoprog Let f(n) be the mean number of flips until achieving n heads in a row. f(0) is clearly 0. In order to get n + 1 many heads in a row, you must first get n many heads in a row. Then you must make one more flip, which has 50-50 odds of success or of making you restart from scratch.


Timestamp: 2020-9-20 5:20:19
Id: 1307550134595706880

@emoprog So f(n + 1) = f(n) + 1 + 0.5 * f(n + 1). This tells us f(n + 1) = 2f(n) + 2. We can quickly calculate f(8) from this, which is 510.

We can even solve this recurrence relation into a closed form, to determine f(n) = 2^(n + 1) - 2 in general.
