Consider …999, extending infinitely left (mirroring how 0.999… extends infinitely right).

In calculating …999 + 1, the first digit rolls over to 0, the carry causes the next digit to roll over to 0, and so on ad infinitum, in a chain reaction eventually making each digit 0.


Timestamp: 2018-2-2 18:6:36
Id: 959488234001584129

Thus, in some sense, …999 + 1 = 0 and thus in some sense …999 = -1.

In similar senses, clearly, 9 + 90 + 900 + … = …999 = -1, and, dividing through by 9, we have 1 + 10 + 100 + … = -1/9.


Timestamp: 2018-2-2 18:6:47
Id: 959488281648906240

Of course, in other more familiar senses, we'd instead say 1 + 10 + 100 + … = +∞. And nothing's wrong with that.


Timestamp: 2018-2-2 18:7:48
Id: 959488538306789376

But still, in different natural contexts and interpretations, we may wish to say 1 + 10 + 100 + … = -1/9.

In dealing with odometers or other such semi-cyclic phenomena, for example, it can be useful to think of 1 + 10 + 100 + … this way.


Timestamp: 2018-2-2 18:8:1
